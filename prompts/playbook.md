# OpenReels Content Playbook

## Hook Patterns

12 proven hook patterns for short-form video. Each pattern has a template, best-fit topic category, and a paired narrative arc.

| # | Pattern | Template | Best For | Paired Arc |
|---|---------|----------|----------|------------|
| 1 | Shocking Statistic | "Did you know that X% of people..." | Facts, data-driven topics | Stat-Escalation |
| 2 | Myth Buster | "Everyone thinks X, but actually..." | Common misconceptions | Myth-Truth |
| 3 | Contrarian Take | "Everything you know about X is wrong." | Counter-intuitive facts | Myth-Truth |
| 4 | Question Hook | "Why does X happen? The answer will surprise you" | Curiosity-driven topics | Revelation |
| 5 | Mistake Callout | "This mistake is costing you..." | Common errors, life hacks | Problem-Solution |
| 6 | Bold Claim | "X changed the world forever" | Historical events, breakthroughs | Tension-Release |
| 7 | Before/After | "Before X existed, we used to Y" | Transformations, progress | Tension-Release |
| 8 | List Preview | "5 things you never knew about X" | Listicles, quick facts | Countdown |
| 9 | Relatable Pain Point | "Are you also struggling with X?" | Current events, shared concerns | Problem-Solution |
| 10 | Time-Bound Urgency | "In just X days, Y is about to happen" | Breaking/upcoming events | Stat-Escalation |
| 11 | Expert Secret | "What experts aren't telling you about X..." | Hidden knowledge, insider info | Revelation |
| 12 | Story Open | "In 1969, a goat herder noticed something strange." | Historical stories, origin tales | Tension-Release |

**How to use:** Evaluate the topic against these 12 patterns. Select the best-fit pattern based on the topic category. Then structure your scenes to follow that pattern's paired narrative arc exactly.

## Narrative Arcs

Each hook pattern maps to one of these 6 scene-by-scene storytelling structures. The arc dictates how scenes flow after the hook. Follow the paired arc for your chosen hook pattern.

### Stat-Escalation Arc
**Paired with:** Shocking Statistic, Time-Bound Urgency

1. **Hook** (3s): Drop the stat or urgency. Make viewers stop scrolling.
2. **Context** (8-10s): Why does this stat matter? What is the background?
3. **Escalation** (10-15s): Reveal deeper, more surprising details that build on the hook.
4. **Implication** (8-10s): So what? What does this mean for the viewer?
5. **CTA** (3-5s): Close with a relevant call-to-action.

### Myth-Truth Arc
**Paired with:** Myth Buster, Contrarian Take

1. **Hook** (3s): State the common belief or myth confidently.
2. **Why people believe it** (8s): Acknowledge why this belief exists.
3. **The truth** (12-15s): Present the actual truth with evidence. Be specific.
4. **What this means** (8s): How should the viewer think differently now?
5. **CTA** (3-5s): Close with a relevant call-to-action.

### Problem-Solution Arc
**Paired with:** Mistake Callout, Relatable Pain Point

1. **Hook** (3s): Name the mistake or pain point. Make viewers feel seen.
2. **Why it's wrong** (10s): Explain the consequences of the mistake.
3. **Correct approach** (12-15s): Present the solution clearly. Step by step if needed.
4. **Benefit** (8s): What does the viewer gain by doing it right?
5. **CTA** (3-5s): Close with a relevant call-to-action.

### Revelation Arc
**Paired with:** Question Hook, Expert Secret

1. **Hook** (3s): Ask the question or tease the secret. Create a curiosity gap.
2. **Build suspense** (10-12s): Add context that makes the answer MORE interesting.
3. **Reveal** (8-10s): Deliver the answer. Make it satisfying and specific.
4. **Deeper implication** (8s): What else does this reveal?
5. **CTA** (3-5s): Close with a relevant call-to-action.

### Countdown Arc
**Paired with:** List Preview

1. **Hook** (3s): Tease the count. "5 things..."
2. **Items in ascending impact** (25-35s): Start with interesting, end with mind-blowing. Each item gets 5-7 seconds.
3. **Strongest item last**: The final item should be the most surprising.
4. **CTA** (3-5s): Close with a relevant call-to-action.

### Tension-Release Arc
**Paired with:** Bold Claim, Before/After, Story Open

1. **Hook** (3s): Make the bold claim or start the story. Create tension.
2. **Build tension** (10-12s): Present evidence, show both sides, or build the stakes.
3. **Evidence/proof** (10-12s): Deliver the proof that resolves the tension.
4. **Resolution** (8s): What does the viewer take away?
5. **CTA** (3-5s): Close with a relevant call-to-action.

## Pacing Rules

These rules are HARD CONSTRAINTS. Violating them produces content that loses viewers.

- **3-second rule**: The hook MUST land in the first 3 seconds. No greetings, no intros, no context setup. Start with the hook immediately.
- **Retention checkpoints**: At the 5s, 15s, and 30s marks, a new scene must begin OR a surprising fact/visual contrast must be introduced.
- **Front-load value**: The most interesting information comes first, not last.
- **Scene duration range**: Each scene should be 3-12 seconds. Never exceed 12 seconds on a single visual.
- **Total duration sweet spot**: 35-50 seconds for quick facts, 45-60 seconds for stories.
- **CTA placement**: Final 3-5 seconds only. Never longer.
- **One idea per scene**: Each scene delivers exactly one piece of information.
- **Spoken rhythm**: Short, punchy sentences in the hook (4-8 words). Slightly longer in explanation (10-15 words). Punchy again for CTA (5-10 words).

## Visual Storytelling Rules

These rules guide image generation for each scene.

- **Show the topic, enrich beyond the words**: The image should visually depict the subject being discussed AND add visual context the narration doesn't cover. If narration mentions a flood, show floodwaters WITH scale (people, buildings for reference), aftermath details, or the specific location.
- **Visual contrast**: Consecutive scenes MUST have visually distinct settings, colors, or compositions. If previous scenes showed daytime or indoor settings, vary the environment.
- **Emotional escalation**: Visuals should intensify alongside the narrative arc. Early scenes can be calmer; later scenes should be more dramatic, detailed, or emotionally charged.
- **No text in images**: Do not include any text, captions, or watermarks in the image. Subtitles are added separately.
- **Vertical composition**: All images are 9:16 (portrait/vertical). Compose with the subject filling the frame vertically.
- **Style adherence**: All scenes must follow the archetype's palette, lighting, and art style consistently.
- **Name real people and places**: If the topic involves well-known public figures, name them explicitly. Same for famous landmarks. AI image generators handle known subjects much better with actual names.

Select the CTA that best matches the content type.

## Music Mood Tags

- **epic_cinematic**: Big, dramatic, orchestral. For stories of triumph, power, or historical moments.
- **chill_lofi**: Relaxed, warm, lo-fi beats. For educational content, reflections, gentle stories.
- **tense_electronic**: Dark, pulsing, synthetic. For tech news, warnings, urgent topics.
- **uplifting_pop**: Bright, energetic, major key. For positive stories, achievements, inspiration.
- **mysterious_ambient**: Ethereal, spacious, uncertain. For mysteries, unexplored topics, philosophical questions.

## Audience Psychology Triggers

Use these psychological triggers naturally in your content.

- **FOMO**: "Most people have no idea..." Makes viewers feel they're getting exclusive knowledge.
- **Curiosity gap**: Always open a loop before closing it. Ask a question or tease information before revealing it.
- **Social currency**: Give viewers something worth sharing. A fact that makes them look smart when they tell friends.
- **Surprise**: Lead with the most unexpected angle. The best reels make viewers stop and say "wait, what?"
- **Authority**: Reference credible sources, studies, or experts to build trust.

## Critic Rubric

The critic evaluates each Short across 7 dimensions using this concrete rubric. Each dimension is scored 1-10.

### Hook Strength (Weight: 20%)
- **1-3**: Generic opening with no recognizable pattern. Slow start.
- **4-6**: Recognizable hook pattern but weak execution.
- **7-8**: Clear hook pattern that lands within 3 seconds. Creates genuine curiosity.
- **9-10**: Irresistible hook. Viewer cannot scroll past.

### Narrative Arc (Weight: 20%)
- **1-3**: No structure. Scenes feel random.
- **4-6**: Some structure but flat middle section.
- **7-8**: Clear arc matching the selected pattern.
- **9-10**: Perfect arc. Each scene earns the next.

### Pacing (Weight: 15%)
- **1-3**: Total script >160 words OR any scene >35 words OR hook >15 words.
- **4-6**: Total script significantly over the tier's word budget OR any scene exceeds the tier's per-scene range OR one scene holds >30% of total words.
- **7-8**: Total words within the tier's budget, all scenes within the tier's per-scene range, hook <=15 words, scene count within the tier's range.
- **9-10**: Meets 7-8 criteria PLUS dynamic rhythm — short-long-short sentence variation, builds energy toward climax.

### Visual Variety (Weight: 15%)
- **1-3**: Same visual_type repeated throughout. Looks like an AI slideshow.
- **4-6**: Some variety but golden rule violations or weak visual contrast.
- **7-8**: Good mix of visual types. Golden rule respected. Visually distinct scenes.
- **9-10**: Perfect variety. Each scene feels like a different shot from a produced video.

### Visual-Narration Sync (Weight: 10%)
- **1-3**: Image prompts contradict narration.
- **4-6**: Loosely related.
- **7-8**: Accurate match with enriching details beyond narration.
- **9-10**: Perfect sync. Visuals add context the narration doesn't cover.

### Style Adherence (Weight: 10%)
- **1-3**: Wildly inconsistent visual styles across scenes.
- **4-6**: Some consistency but noticeable drift from archetype.
- **7-8**: All scenes follow the archetype's palette, lighting, and art style.
- **9-10**: Perfect cohesion. Every scene feels like it belongs to the same video.

### CTA Effectiveness (Weight: 10%)
- **1-3**: No CTA or generic "subscribe".
- **4-6**: Weak connection to content.
- **7-8**: Matches content type from CTA Pattern Library.
- **9-10**: Creates urgency and matches psychology triggers.

### Scoring Formula
Overall score = round(Hook*0.20 + Arc*0.20 + Pacing*0.15 + Variety*0.15 + Sync*0.10 + Style*0.10 + CTA*0.10)

Pass threshold: 7
